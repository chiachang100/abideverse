# AbideVerse Study Guides

These guides are designed to help you move from _reading_ Scripture to _abiding_ in it. Each guide follows the same pattern—prayer, observation, interpretation, reflection, and action.

## How to Use a Study Guide

1. **Choose a passage** (one chapter or 10–20 verses maximum).
2. **Read the passage twice** – once silently, once aloud.
3. **Work through the sections** in order. Do not skip the “Pause & Pray” step.
4. **Write your answers** (digital or paper journal). Abiding requires slow thought.
5. **Share one insight** with another person before your next study session.

## Template Structure

Every guide follows this markdown layout:

# [Book Chapter: Verses] – [Short Title]

## 🕊️ Pause & Pray

_Ask God to quiet your heart and open your understanding._

## 📖 Read the Passage

> _Quote the key verses (or link to passage)_

## 🔍 Observation – What does it say?

- **Who** is speaking / being addressed?
- **What** happens or is commanded?
- **Where** / **When** (historical context)?
- **Repetitions** – any word or phrase repeated?

## 💡 Interpretation – What does it mean?

- **Key terms** (look up one Hebrew/Greek word using a tool like Blue Letter Bible)
- **Cross references** (find 1–2 other verses that echo this theme)
- **Cultural note** (what would the original hearer have understood?)

## ❤️ Reflection – What does it mean for me?

- Does this passage comfort, confront, or confuse me? Why?
- Write a 1‑sentence summary of the main point in your own words.

## 🚶 Action – How will I abide?

- **One concrete change** this week (e.g., “I will speak gently to a difficult coworker”)
- **Memory verse** (write out the key verse on an index card)
- **Pray back the passage** (turn the text into a prayer)
