# Acknowledgements

The _AbideVerse_ project exists because of the grace, truth, and community that have shaped its foundation. We are deeply grateful to everyone who made this work possible.

## Spiritual & Theological Foundations

- **The Author of Truth**: Above all, we thank God—Father, Son, and Holy Spirit—for the living Word that abides forever. _Scriptura sacra et efficax_.
- **Faithful Translators & Textual Scholars**: From the Masoretes and Septuagint translators to the committees behind the ESV, KJV, NASB, and CSB—your labor gives us access to the Scriptures in our own tongue.
- **Church Fathers, Reformers, and Pastors**: Augustine, Athanasius, Luther, Calvin, Wesley, and countless unnamed shepherds whose expositions still echo in these study guides.

## Research & Study Resources

- **Lexicons & Dictionaries** – _BDAG_, _HALOT_, _Strong’s Concordance_, _Mounce’s Greek Dictionary_.
- **Commentary Sets** – _NICOT/NICNT_, _Baker Exegetical_, _Tyndale_, _Matthew Henry’s Concise_, and _The IVP Bible Background Commentary_.
- **Digital Tools** – The open-source community behind _Logos_ (indexing standards), _STEP Bible_, _Blue Letter Bible_, and _e-Sword_ for API and cross-reference logic.
- **Academic Open Access** – _Atla Religion Database_, _The Gospel Coalition_, _Desiring God_, and _BiblicalTraining.org_ for theological frameworks.

## Community Contributors

- **Beta Readers & Small Groups** – The twelve test groups across three continents who stress‑tested the _Study Guides_ and gave candid feedback.
- **Design & Accessibility Reviewers** – Those who ensured the verse notes and fonts are usable for neurodivergent readers, seniors, and second‑language English speakers.
- **Technical Support** – Volunteers who helped format `.md` templates, build the internal cross‑reference tagger, and maintain the _AbideVerse_ GitHub repository.
